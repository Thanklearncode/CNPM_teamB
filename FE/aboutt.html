Koi Fish - Symbol of Luck and Prosperity

Introduction to Koi Fish

Koi (錦鯉, Nishikigoi) is a bred variety of carp originating from Japan. Known as the "Emperor of ornamental fish", Koi is famous not only for its magnificent beauty but also for its deep cultural significance.

Common Koi Varieties

  • Kohaku (white with red)
  • Sanke (white with red and black)
  • Showa (black with red and white)
  • Tanchō (white with red dot on head)
  • Asagi (blue with red)

Notable Features

  • Long lifespan (25-35 years)
  • Large size (60-90cm)
  • Diverse colors
  • Gentle temperament
  • Easy to raise and care for

Care Guide

  • Ideal water temperature: 15-25°C
  • Water pH: 6.8-7.2
  • Feed 2-3 times/day
  • Regular water changes and pond cleaning
  • Regular health checks
  • Avoid overfeeding

Feng Shui Significance

In Feng Shui, Koi fish represents:

  • Success and prosperity
  • Luck and fortune
  • Strength and perseverance
  • Love and happy marriage
